Q1. What is AyushCare program?
A- AyushCare is a health tracker program for senior citizens delivered at the comfort of their home. It includes periodic home visit by a Family Doctor and routine pathology tests with entire program monitored by Medical specialists. Electronic Medical Record available over the web, enables all interested parties to be fully informed at all times about the status of their near and dear ones irrespective of their geographical location
Q2. What does the package aim to deliver?
A- Nearly 7.5% of India's population is aged 60 years and above and many will have disorders such as Diabetes, Heart disease, Bowel disorders, Arthritis, Alzheimer's, Depression, Fall resulting in fractures, etc. needing regular medical attention at home.

AyushCare is a health tracker program for senior citizens delivered at their doorstep, through periodic home visit by a Family Doctor and sample collection for routine pathology tests. This ensures minimal requirement of commuting for receiving medical attention. Entire program is monitored by Medical Specialists belonging to appropriate disciplines. Central to the program is creation of Electronic Medical Records by the doctors consisting of examination details, prescriptions and reports. Children living away from their parents can sponsor, so they get complete access to Electronic Medical Records and be fully informed at all times about the status of their parents irrespective of their geographical location.
Q3. What is the target audience?
A- Primarily, senior citizens, who live by themselves, away from their children. It especially addresses challenges faced by NRIs whose parents live in India.
Q4. What are the major benefits?
1. Physical examination by a qualified doctor at regular intervals.
2. Continuous tracking of vital parameters such as blood sugar, blood pressure, etc.
3. Identification, if any, of chronic problems and implementation of attendant life style modifications
4. India online Health Specialists Panel is available to those who need expert care
5. All health tracking activities delivered at home, to the extent possible
6. Continuous updated health status of the senior citizens(beneficiary) made available over the web to the doctors and subscriber
7. Ability to consult specialists over the web using a video camera
8. Continuity in healthcare can be maintained even if the beneficiary is traveling away from home. He can continue to interact over video with his Family doctor. His EMR can be accessed from anywhere in the globe.
9. Preventive care minimizes hospitalization. Continuity in health care is ensured in case there is a need for hospitalization as EMR is made available to the hospital doctors
Q5. Does the service guarantee that the EMR of the beneficiary is an accurate reflection of his health status?
A- It can be made reasonably accurate if there is active, continuous and complete participation from the beneficiary regarding his medical history and current symptoms. Medical history must be completed in all respects during the registration process by the beneficiary. This along with the information provided by the beneficiary to his AyushCare physician on a continuous basis is the foundation of an accurate record of his health status. The diagnostic tests carried out under the program are essential and vital tests. Further tests will be only recommended (extra costs) based on the Tests conducted and symptomatic inputs provided by the beneficiary.
Q6. Does the package handle acute conditions?
A- No, the package tracks health status and offers advice on preventive steps which will minimize occurrence of acute conditions that may necessitate hospitalization. However, continuous health tracking and availability of Medical record enables more efficient hospital care.
Q7. What if the beneficiary is in need of continuous care from specialists?
A- Routine problems will be handled by the Family doctor under the existing AyushCare program. In case the specialist panel feels the need for intensive care, they will offer such an advice. In any case, any activity not covered by this program will have to be undertaken by the beneficiary at extra cost. AyushCare program will continue concurrently and the AyushCare visiting physician will continue to track health status/progress.
Q8. If the beneficiary is a chronic patient, will AyushCare package manage his requirements?
A- If the problems that beneficiary has can be managed with once a month visit of the physician (not a specialist) then he may not need to seek help outside of AyushCare. AyushCare package may not cover all his requirements if he has some serious problems that necessitates frequent visit to his doctor.He can certainly use INDIAONLINEHEALTH platform to manage his requirements at discounted rates. Also it will ensure continuity in his healthcare
Q9. What are the deliverables?
A- See plan details for what you have opted for. Some of the key deliverables are; Periodic home visit by a Family doctor, basic pathology tests, Sonography, visit of the beneficiary to the specialist clinic for consultation (or over a video link if the beneficiary is unable to travel), and annual review by the specialist, access to EMR by authorized personnel over the web, complimentary subscription to IndiaOnlineHealth services (ensure continuity in healthcare at all times), email response to queries from the subscriber and beneficiary, and EMR in paper form provided is if the beneficiary is hospitalized.
Q10. What are the roles and responsibilities of Ayushman Pvt. Ltd.?
1. Provide a platform for Creation and maintenance of EMR
2. Ensure home visit of a doctor as per Service Level agreements
3. Ensure review of EMR by a specialist and generation of an advice for chronic care management
4. A panel of specialist registered with IndiaOnlineHealth are made available ensuring continuity in health care
5. Family doctor will answer email queries from subscribers about the health status of the beneficiary
6. Accredited pathology lab and registered with IndiaOnlineHealth and associated with AyushCare program will collect sample from home conduct tests and file reports online as per SLA. Additional tests can be ordered over the web at discounted rates
7. Medical record will be made available to hospitals upon request, in case of hospitalization
Q11. What are the roles and responsibilities of the subscriber (NRI)?
1. Register the beneficiary and help him create his medical history.
2. Ensure it is accurate and complete, especially demographic details, allergy etc.
3. Ensure that the subscription amount is paid in advance and account is kept active at all times.
4. Ensure that the beneficiary (parent) adheres to the schedule set and agreed to in terms of availability at home during the visit of doctor and pathology center person who is responsible for collecting samples.
5. Go thru the beneficiary EMR periodically and if need be bring it to the attention of AyushCare staff via email/phone. If the beneficiary needs intensive care as determined by AyushCare specialists, ensure such care is provided that falls outside purview of AyushCare package.
AyushCare program will continue to support the beneficiary at this time as per SLA agreed to.
Q12. What are the roles and responsibilities of the beneficiary (Parent)?
1. Ensure that the medical history provided is accurate and complete at all times.
2. If he is availing of any medical services outside of IndiaOnlineHealth, ensure that the EMR is updated and bring it to the attention of AyushCare physician.
3. He should make himself available to the AyushCare staff at the scheduled time and place (home or clinic or diagnostic center) so that the services can be rendered efficiently.
4. If he wishes to reschedule the appointment he should do so well in advance but in no case later than 48 hours, in advance.
5. Follow all instructions given by the Family doctor in letter and spirit.

Q18. Can the Beneficiary/Subscriber choose the doctor who will visit the Beneficiary and who is responsible for the program?
A- Yes he may do so subject to his geographical location. All the visiting physicians under this program are carefully chosen and undergo training in handling health of elderly patients. Since they will be visiting Beneficiary's home, their travel time is optimized by allocating visit locations that are close by. The visiting physician becomes beneficiary's close friend and confidant for all of his health needs. AyushCare staff will take every care to assign the same person (as far as practical) for the duration of the program.
Q19. Will the Family doctor visit the Beneficiary's home or the Beneficiary needs to go the doctor's clinic?
A- The designated family doctor will visit the Beneficiary home every as per plan selected, and normally schedule is set at the beginning of the program. The beneficiary should make himself available at his home at the designated time. For routine Pathology tests the designated lab will send their representative to the beneficiary home to collect the sample at the agreed time.
Q20. Will the Specialist will visit beneficiary's home or he will have to see him at the clinic?
A- Beneficiary needs to visit the specialist at his clinic. AyushCare will help fix the meeting and it is important for the Beneficiary to keep the appointment. Under AyushCare-Advance plan the Beneficiary will be picked up and taken to the Specialists clinic. Under other plans this service can be availed of at extra cost.
Q21. What if the Beneficiary is not available at the appointed hour or the doctor does not turn up?
A- It is essential that the Beneficiary is available for examination at his home at the appointed date and time. However if he desires a change and requests AyushCare staff for rescheduling 48 hours prior to the actual engagement, every effort will be made to accommodate. If the schedule does not permit, the AyushCare staff may set-up a meeting at the Family doctor's clinic. The Beneficiary needs to present himself at the clinic at the appointed time.
Q22. Can the beneficiary choose the date and time of the Family doctor's visit?
A- It's not always possible as the Family doctor has tight schedule. The overheads of travel time etc. does not permit such a thing. However the Beneficiary will be consulted when the visit schedule is drawn up.
